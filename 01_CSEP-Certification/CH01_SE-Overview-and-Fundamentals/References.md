# CSEP Week 1 — Additional References & Standards
**Purpose:** Supplementary reading list beyond the INCOSE SEH 5th Edition for Week 1 topics.
All references below are directly relevant to CSEP exam topics covered in Week 1.

---

## 🏆 Tier 1 — PRIMARY (Must-Know Standards)
*These are foundational to the CSEP exam and directly referenced in SEH5.*

---

### 1. ISO/IEC/IEEE 15288:2023
**Full Title:** Systems and software engineering — System life cycle processes
**Publisher:** ISO / IEC / IEEE (joint standard)
**Edition:** 2023 (3rd edition)
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — The foundational standard on which SEH5 is based
**What it covers:**
- All 30 system life cycle processes across 4 process groups
- Process purpose, outcomes, and activities for each process
- Guidance on tailoring
- Alignment with ISO 15289 (information items) and ISO 21505 (project management)
**Week 1 Topics Covered:** Life cycle stages, all four process groups, technical processes, technical management processes, agreement processes
**How to Access:**
- Purchase from [ISO Store](https://www.iso.org/standard/81702.html)
- Access through IEEE Xplore (institutional subscription)
- Some university libraries provide free access
**Study Tip:** For the CSEP exam, you don't need to read every clause in detail. Focus on the *purpose* and *outcomes* of each process. The Purpose/Outcomes structure maps directly to exam questions.

---

### 2. INCOSE Systems Engineering Handbook, 5th Edition (SEH5)
**Full Title:** INCOSE Systems Engineering Handbook: A Guide for System Life Cycle Processes and Activities
**Authors:** David D. Walden, Garry J. Roedler, Kevin Forsberg, R. Douglas Hamelin, Thomas M. Shortell (Editors)
**Publisher:** INCOSE / Wiley, 2023
**ISBN:** 978-1-119-81429-0
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — THE primary exam reference (as of March 15, 2025, all CSEP questions are based on SEH5)
**What it covers:**
- Parts I & II: SE concepts, life cycle stages, and processes
- Part III: Tailoring and application
- Part IV: SE in practice (MBSE, Agile, SoS, etc.)
- Appendices: N2 diagrams, process relationships
**Where to Purchase:** [Wiley](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/INCOSE+Systems+Engineering+Handbook,+5th+Edition-p-9781119814290) | INCOSE member discount available
**Study Tip:** INCOSE members receive a discount. If you're not a member, consider joining — membership also includes access to working groups and study resources.

---

### 3. ISO/IEC/IEEE 29148:2018
**Full Title:** Systems and software engineering — Life cycle processes — Requirements engineering
**Publisher:** ISO / IEC / IEEE (joint standard)
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Critical for requirements-related exam questions
**What it covers:**
- Requirements elicitation, analysis, specification, verification, and management
- Qualities of good requirements (the authoritative list)
- Template for Systems Requirements Specification (SyRS)
- Template for Stakeholder Requirements Specification (StRS)
- ConOps content guidance
**Week 1 Topics Covered:** Day 4 — Stakeholder Needs, System Requirements Definition
**Study Tip:** Know the requirement qualities table from this standard — the exam uses this taxonomy for "what makes a good requirement" questions.

---

### 4. ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010:2022
**Full Title:** Software, systems and enterprise — Architecture description
**Publisher:** ISO / IEC / IEEE
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Architecture definition process questions
**What it covers:**
- Formal definitions of architecture, architecture description, viewpoints, views, and models
- Framework for architecture documentation
- Correspondence rules between views
**Week 1 Topics Covered:** Day 4 — Architecture Definition Process
**Study Tip:** Know the viewpoint/view distinction. The exam tests the formal ISO 42010 definitions.

## 🥈 Tier 2 — SECONDARY (Highly Recommended)
*These are not exam primary references but provide deep understanding and real-world context.*

---

### 5. NASA Systems Engineering Handbook (NASA/SP-2016-6105 Rev 2)
**Full Title:** NASA Systems Engineering Handbook
**Publisher:** NASA, 2016
**Access:** Free PDF — [NASA Technical Reports Server](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20170001761)
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Excellent real-world application of SE principles
**What it covers:**
- SE principles applied to NASA mission contexts
- Requirements, architecture, verification/validation guidance
- SE Management Plan templates
- Life cycle review (milestone) descriptions
**Why Read It:** NASA's handbook is exceptionally well-written and provides concrete examples of how SE processes work in a real high-stakes environment. The milestone review descriptions (SRR, PDR, CDR, etc.) are clearer here than in any standard.
**Week 1 Topics Covered:** All days — provides real-world grounding

---

### 6. DAU Systems Engineering Guidebook / Defense Acquisition Guidebook
**Publisher:** Defense Acquisition University (DAU), US DoD
**Access:** Free — [DAU](https://www.dau.edu/) and [DAU Acquipedia](https://www.dau.edu/acquipedia)
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ — DoD context, useful if working in defence
**What it covers:**
- DoD Acquisition lifecycle (Milestones A, B, C)
- SE in defence acquisition context
- Technical reviews (SRR, PDR, CDR, TRR, etc.)
- Risk management in DoD programmes

---

### 7. SEBoK — Systems Engineering Body of Knowledge
**Full Title:** Guide to the Systems Engineering Body of Knowledge
**Publisher:** INCOSE / BKCASE Project
**Access:** Free — [sebokwiki.org](https://sebokwiki.org)
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Comprehensive free reference, exam-aligned
**What it covers:**
- All SE knowledge areas aligned with INCOSE and ISO 15288
- Context-specific SE application guides
- Links to primary standards and research
**Study Tip:** Use SEBoK as a free alternative explanation when SEH5 concepts aren't clear. The SEBoK wiki is free and comprehensive.

---

### 8. MIL-STD-499C (Draft)
**Full Title:** Systems Engineering (Draft — not yet formally released)
**Publisher:** US Department of Defense
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐ — Background reading for defence practitioners
**Note:** MIL-STD-499B was cancelled in 1994 and replaced by commercial standards (ISO 15288). The draft 499C exists but has not been formally issued. References to 499-series in context provide historical perspective on how DoD adopted commercial SE practices.

---

### 9. ANSI/EIA-632:2003
**Full Title:** Processes for Engineering a System
**Publisher:** Electronic Industries Alliance (EIA)
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐ — Historical context; predecessor to ISO 15288
**What it covers:** An early U.S. standard for SE processes, now largely superseded by ISO 15288. Understanding it provides context for how the industry moved toward international standards.

## 🥉 Tier 3 — SUPPLEMENTARY (Deeper Dives)

---

### 10. ISO 31000:2018 — Risk Management
**Full Title:** Risk management — Guidelines
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ — Underpins the Risk Management Process
**What it covers:** International standard for risk management principles, framework, and process. Referenced by SE risk management practice.
**Access:** Purchase from [ISO Store](https://www.iso.org/standard/65694.html)

---

### 11. ISO/IEC/IEEE 15289:2019
**Full Title:** Systems and software engineering — Content of life-cycle information items (documentation)
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ — Companion to ISO 15288
**What it covers:** Specifies the required content of all documentation produced during the system life cycle — ConOps, SyRS, architecture descriptions, test plans, etc.

---

### 12. ISO/IEC 25010:2023 — Product Quality
**Full Title:** Systems and software engineering — Systems and software Quality Requirements and Evaluation (SQuaRE) — Product quality model
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐ — Quality characteristics
**What it covers:** Defines software and system quality characteristics including functionality, reliability, usability, efficiency, maintainability, portability, and security.

---

### 13. IEEE 1220 (Superseded by ISO 15288)
**Historical Note:** IEEE 1220 (Standard for Application and Management of the Systems Engineering Process) was a US standard that predated ISO 15288. It was withdrawn in 2012. Awareness of its existence is useful for historical context only.

---

### 14. DODAF 2.02 — DoD Architecture Framework
**Full Title:** Department of Defense Architecture Framework Version 2.02
**Publisher:** US DoD
**Access:** Free — [DoD CIO](https://dodcio.defense.gov/Library/DoD-Architecture-Framework/)
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ — Architecture Definition questions (Day 4)
**What it covers:** Defines architecture viewpoints (All, Capability, Services, Operational, Systems, Technical, Programme, Data & Information, Project) and their associated products (models, diagrams).

## 📚 Textbooks & Books (Non-Standard)

---

### 15. "Systems Engineering and Analysis" — Blanchard & Fabrycky
**Authors:** Benjamin S. Blanchard & Wolter J. Fabrycky
**Publisher:** Pearson, 5th Edition (2011)
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Classic SE textbook, widely used in university SE courses
**Why Read:** Provides extensive worked examples of requirements analysis, life cycle costing, trade studies, and system effectiveness modelling.

---

### 16. "Thinking in Systems" — Donella Meadows
**Author:** Donella H. Meadows
**Publisher:** Chelsea Green Publishing (2008)
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Systems thinking foundation (Day 2)
**Why Read:** The most accessible introduction to systems thinking. Covers feedback loops, stocks and flows, leverage points. Relevant to the systems thinking principles tested in Week 1.

---

### 17. "The Art of Systems Architecting" — Maier & Rechtin
**Authors:** Mark W. Maier & Eberhardt Rechtin
**Publisher:** CRC Press, 3rd Edition (2009)
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ — Architecture (Day 4)
**Why Read:** Introduces heuristics-based approach to system architecting — a complementary perspective to the standards-based view.

---

### 18. "Model-Based Systems Engineering" — Estefan
**Author:** Jeff A. Estefan
**Publisher:** INCOSE (2007, free survey paper)
**Access:** Search "Survey of MBSE Methodologies Estefan INCOSE"
**Relevance:** ⭐⭐ — Preview of Week 2 MBSE content
