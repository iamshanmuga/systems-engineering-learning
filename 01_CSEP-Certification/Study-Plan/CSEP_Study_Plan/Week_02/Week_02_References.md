# CSEP Week 2 — References: Life Cycle Concepts
**Chapter:** INCOSE SEH5 Chapter 2 — Life Cycle Concepts

## 🏆 Tier 1 — PRIMARY (Must-Know Standards)

### ISO/IEC/IEEE 15288:2023 — Systems and Software Engineering: System Life Cycle Processes
- **Author/Publisher:** ISO / IEC / IEEE | 3rd Edition | 2023
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **What it covers:**
  - The definitive standard for life cycle stages (§6.2) and the 30 processes (§6.1–§6.4)
  - Decision gate principles and stage transition requirements
  - Process group definitions (Technical, Technical Management, Agreement, Organisational)
  - Tailoring guidance for applying processes to specific programmes
- **Week 02 Topics Covered:** All six life cycle stages; decision gates; all four process groups; processes vs stages distinction
- **How to Access:** ISO Online Browsing Platform (paid); IEEE Xplore (institutional access); check if your organisation holds a licence
- **Study Tip:** Focus on §6.2 (life cycle stages) and the introductory §6.1 (process groups overview) this week; do not get drawn into the individual process clauses yet — that is Week 3 onward.

---

### INCOSE Systems Engineering Handbook, 5th Edition (SEH5)
- **Author/Publisher:** INCOSE | Wiley | 5th Edition | 2023
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **What it covers:**
  - Chapter 2: Life Cycle Concepts — the primary CSEP exam source for this week
  - Life cycle stages with practical descriptions of activities, artefacts, and decision criteria
  - Life cycle models and tailoring guidance
  - Cost of change discussion (citing Boehm)
- **Week 02 Topics Covered:** All Day 1–6 topics
- **How to Access:** Purchase from Wiley (print or eBook); INCOSE members receive discounted access; available via institutional library catalogues
- **Study Tip:** Read Chapter 2 in full this week — it is shorter than the technical process chapters but contains high-density CSEP exam content on stages and models.

## 🥈 Tier 2 — SECONDARY (Highly Recommended)

### SEBoK v2.10 — Systems Engineering Body of Knowledge
- **Author/Publisher:** BKCASE Editorial Board | Continuously updated
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **What it covers:**
  - Part 4: Applications of Systems Engineering — product/service life cycle sections
  - Knowledge Area: "Life Cycle Models" with summaries of sequential, iterative, concurrent, and evolutionary models
  - Relationship between life cycle stages and SE processes
- **Week 02 Topics Covered:** Life cycle models (Day 4); processes vs stages (Day 5)
- **How to Access:** Free at sebokwiki.org — search "Life Cycle Models" knowledge area
- **Study Tip:** Use the SEBoK "Life Cycle Models" article as a concise comparison of all four model types before attempting practice questions.

---

### NASA Systems Engineering Handbook (NASA/SP-2016-6105 Rev2)
- **Author/Publisher:** NASA | 2016 | Free PDF
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **What it covers:**
  - Chapter 1: NASA's life cycle and life cycle reviews
  - Formal named reviews (SRR, MDR, PDR, CDR, SAR, ORR, FRR, PLAR) mapped to NASA life cycle phases
  - Strong practical illustrations of decision gate concepts
- **Week 02 Topics Covered:** Decision gates (Day 3); life cycle stages (Day 2)
- **How to Access:** Free PDF — NASA Technical Reports Server (NTRS); search "NASA/SP-2016-6105"
- **Study Tip:** Use NASA's named review table as a concrete illustration of decision gates — the CSEP exam does not require NASA-specific knowledge but the concepts map directly to ISO 15288.

---

### DAU Systems Engineering Fundamentals Guide
- **Author/Publisher:** Defense Acquisition University (DAU) | US DoD
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐
- **What it covers:**
  - Defence acquisition life cycle overview and alignment with ISO 15288 stages
  - Milestone decision reviews (Milestones A, B, C) as an example of decision gate implementation
  - Cost of change discussion in a defence acquisition context
- **Week 02 Topics Covered:** Decision gates (Day 3); cost of change (Day 6)
- **How to Access:** Free at dau.edu — search "Systems Engineering Fundamentals"
- **Study Tip:** Use DAU's Milestones A/B/C as a real-world example of decision gates; note how they map to (but do not exactly equal) ISO 15288 stage boundaries.

## 🥉 Tier 3 — SUPPLEMENTARY (Deeper Dives)

### "Managing the Development of Large Software Systems" — Barry Boehm (1981)
- **Author/Publisher:** Barry W. Boehm | IEEE WESCON Proceedings | 1981
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐
- **What it covers:**
  - Original publication of the cost-of-change research underpinning the cost curve cited in SEH5
  - Introduces the concept of cost multiplication factors for late defect correction
- **Week 02 Topics Covered:** Cost of change (Day 6)
- **How to Access:** IEEE Xplore (institutional); various free secondary sources cite the core data
- **Study Tip:** You do not need to read the original paper for CSEP — but knowing that Boehm is the source of the cost curve is useful if the exam asks about the research basis.

---

### ISO/IEC/IEEE 24748 (Parts 1–4) — Life Cycle Management
- **Author/Publisher:** ISO / IEC / IEEE | Multiple parts
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐
- **What it covers:**
  - Part 1: Guidelines for life cycle management (expands on ISO 15288 §6.2)
  - Part 2: Guidelines for the application of ISO 15288 and ISO 12207
  - Detailed guidance on tailoring life cycle models and applying processes
- **Week 02 Topics Covered:** Life cycle models (Day 4); life cycle model tailoring
- **How to Access:** ISO store (paid); check institutional access
- **Study Tip:** Skim Part 1 only if you want deeper tailoring guidance; not required for CSEP pass standard but useful for professional practice.

---

### "Thinking in Systems: A Primer" — Donella Meadows (2008)
- **Author/Publisher:** Donella H. Meadows | Chelsea Green Publishing | 2008
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐
- **What it covers:**
  - Systems thinking as a complement to life cycle thinking
  - Feedback loop dynamics relevant to understanding operational and support stage behaviour
- **Week 02 Topics Covered:** Background context for life cycle thinking (Day 1)
- **How to Access:** Widely available — bookshops, Amazon, library catalogues
- **Study Tip:** Optional reading this week; more directly useful for Week 1 review. Meadows was covered in Week 1 flashcards.
