# CSEP Week 3 — References: Business/Mission Analysis & Stakeholder Needs
**Chapter:** INCOSE SEH5 Chapter 3 — Technical Processes (Part 1A)
**ISO Anchor:** ISO/IEC/IEEE 15288:2023 §6.4.1 (Business or Mission Analysis) and §6.4.2 (Stakeholder Needs and Requirements Definition)
**Companion Standard:** ISO/IEC/IEEE 29148:2018 — Requirements Engineering

---

## 🏆 Tier 1 — PRIMARY (Must-Know Standards)

### ISO/IEC/IEEE 15288:2023 — Systems and Software Engineering: System Life Cycle Processes
- **Author/Publisher:** ISO / IEC / IEEE | 3rd Edition | 2023
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **What it covers:**
  - §6.4.1 Business or Mission Analysis — purpose, outcomes, activities, inputs, outputs
  - §6.4.2 Stakeholder Needs and Requirements Definition — purpose, outcomes, activities, inputs, outputs
  - The formal placement of both processes within the 14 Technical Processes (§6.4)
  - Tailoring guidance for applying these processes to specific programmes
- **Week 03 Topics Covered:** Every Day 1–7 topic; authoritative source for process definitions and terminology
- **How to Access:** ISO Online Browsing Platform (paid); IEEE Xplore (institutional access); check if your organisation holds a licence
- **Study Tip:** Read §6.4.1 and §6.4.2 in full this week. Pay close attention to the "Outcomes" sub-clauses — these define what "done" looks like for each process and are high-yield CSEP exam material.

---

### INCOSE Systems Engineering Handbook, 5th Edition (SEH5)
- **Author/Publisher:** INCOSE | Wiley | 5th Edition | 2023
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **What it covers:**
  - Chapter 3: Technical Processes — the primary CSEP exam source for this week
  - BMA and SN&RD sections with practical descriptions of activities, artefacts, and entry/exit criteria
  - Relationships between BMA, SN&RD, and downstream System Requirements Definition
  - Worked examples of ConOps and stakeholder requirements
- **Week 03 Topics Covered:** All Day 1–7 topics
- **How to Access:** Purchase from Wiley (print or eBook); INCOSE members receive discounted access; available via institutional library catalogues
- **Study Tip:** Read the BMA and SN&RD sections of Chapter 3 in full this week. Skim the remaining Technical Processes sections — they are the material for Week 4 onwards.

---

### ISO/IEC/IEEE 29148:2018 — Systems and Software Engineering: Life Cycle Processes — Requirements Engineering
- **Author/Publisher:** ISO / IEC / IEEE | 2nd Edition | 2018
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **What it covers:**
  - §4.2 Concept of Operations (ConOps) — definition, content, viewpoint
  - §4.3 Operational Concept (OpsCon) — definition, content, viewpoint
  - §5.2 Requirement characteristics — the nine characteristics of a good requirement
  - §5.3 Requirements engineering activities and work products
  - Templates for stakeholder requirements, system requirements, and interface requirements
- **Week 03 Topics Covered:** ConOps vs OpsCon (Day 2); requirement characteristics (Day 5); requirements hierarchy (Day 5)
- **How to Access:** ISO Online Browsing Platform (paid); IEEE Xplore (institutional access)
- **Study Tip:** 29148 is the single most important standard *after* 15288 for this week. Memorise §5.2 — the nine characteristics are near-certain exam material, typically tested by asking you to classify a defective requirement.

### NASA Systems Engineering Handbook (NASA/SP-2016-6105 Rev2)
- **Author/Publisher:** NASA | 2016 | Free PDF
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **What it covers:**
  - Chapter 4 §4.1: Stakeholder Expectations Definition (NASA's equivalent of SN&RD)
  - Chapter 4 §4.2: Technical Requirements Definition (preparation for Week 4)
  - Appendix C: ConOps template with worked examples
  - Strong illustrations of stakeholder mapping and expectation capture
- **Week 03 Topics Covered:** ConOps (Day 2); stakeholder identification and elicitation (Day 4); requirements hierarchy (Day 5)
- **How to Access:** Free PDF — NASA Technical Reports Server (NTRS); search "NASA/SP-2016-6105"
- **Study Tip:** NASA calls the process "Stakeholder Expectations Definition" rather than SN&RD. The concepts map directly to ISO 15288 §6.4.2 but the terminology differs — a potential exam trap if you only study NASA.

---

### DAU Systems Engineering Fundamentals Guide
- **Author/Publisher:** Defense Acquisition University (DAU) | US DoD
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐
- **What it covers:**
  - Capabilities-Based Assessment (CBA) — defence-flavoured BMA
  - Initial Capabilities Document (ICD) and Capability Development Document (CDD) as BMA and SN&RD outputs
  - Stakeholder analysis techniques in an acquisition context
- **Week 03 Topics Covered:** BMA activities (Day 2); SN&RD outputs (Day 3); stakeholder identification (Day 4)
- **How to Access:** Free at dau.edu — search "Systems Engineering Fundamentals" and "Capabilities-Based Assessment"
- **Study Tip:** Good for concrete examples of BMA and SN&RD outputs in a defence acquisition context. The CSEP exam uses generic ISO 15288 terminology, so treat DAU terms as illustrative — do not substitute them in exam answers.

---

### INCOSE Guide for Writing Requirements (GtWR)
- **Author/Publisher:** INCOSE Requirements Working Group | Latest edition 2023
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **What it covers:**
  - Detailed rules and patterns for writing each of the ISO 29148 §5.2 characteristics
  - Before-and-after examples of defective versus conforming requirements
  - Guidance on the "shall" statement convention and forbidden vague terms
- **Week 03 Topics Covered:** Requirement characteristics (Day 5); ambiguity elimination (Day 5)
- **How to Access:** INCOSE members via incose.org; some extracts are freely available
- **Study Tip:** The GtWR is the most practical companion to ISO 29148 §5.2. If you work through even 10 before/after examples from GtWR, your ability to classify defective requirements on the exam improves dramatically.

### "Mastering the Requirements Process" — Suzanne and James Robertson (3rd Ed, 2012)
- **Author/Publisher:** Suzanne Robertson, James Robertson | Addison-Wesley | 3rd Edition | 2012
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐
- **What it covers:**
  - Volere requirements process and shell template
  - Deep coverage of elicitation techniques, stakeholder workshops, and scenario writing
  - Requirement quality gateway concept — practical complement to ISO 29148 §5.2
- **Week 03 Topics Covered:** Elicitation (Day 4); stakeholder analysis (Day 4); requirement quality (Day 5)

### "Writing Better Requirements" — Ian Alexander & Richard Stevens (2002)
- **Author/Publisher:** Ian Alexander, Richard Stevens | Addison-Wesley | 2002
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐

- **How to Access:** Secondhand market; some institutional libraries

### "Use Cases: Requirements in Context" — Daryl Kulak & Eamonn Guiney (2nd Ed, 2003)
- **Author/Publisher:** Daryl Kulak, Eamonn Guiney | Addison-Wesley | 2nd Edition | 2003
- **Relevance:** ⭐⭐
- **What it covers:**
  - Use case authoring as an elicitation and specification technique
  - Relationship between use cases, scenarios, and requirements
- **Week 03 Topics Covered:** Use cases and scenarios as elicitation techniques (Day 4)

## 🔗 Cross-Week Dependency Notes

**Forward dependencies** — Week 03 outputs that feed Week 04 and beyond:
- **Stakeholder requirements baseline** — the input to System Requirements Definition (§6.4.3, Week 04)
- **ConOps / OpsCon** — reused as validation context throughout the programme
- **MoEs** — reused in Validation (§6.4.12) and programme measurement
- **Validation criteria** — carried forward into the Validation process

**Backward dependencies** — Week 02 concepts Week 03 builds on:
- Life cycle stages (Concept Stage is where BMA primarily executes)
- Process vs stage distinction (BMA and SN&RD are *processes*, not stages)
- Decision gates (BMA and SN&RD outputs feed the first programme decision gate)
